How it works

Heuristics first. The model only where it earns its keep. Then prove it on real data.

Most of the work is cheap, deterministic, and offline. The expensive judgment is reserved for the genuinely ambiguous cases — and even then, nothing commits until the join actually executes.

1

Heuristics-first confidence proxy

Value overlap, distribution alignment, entropy, cardinality and key-uniqueness fuse into a [0,1] proxy with an evidence trail. The distribution-divergence check is the discriminator that kills “looks-similar, isn't-related.”

2

Selective LLM adjudication

Confident relationships skip the vote. Only genuinely ambiguous ones go to a diverse ensemble of reasoning paths — schema, value, business-logic — that votes on the relationship type by plurality. Consensus commits; conflict routes to a human.

3

Execute-the-join validation

Before anything is asserted, the candidate join is synthesized and run against the real data: match rate, orphans, fan-out, null keys. An execution that contradicts the inferred type vetoes it — even a unanimous vote can't override the data.

4

Typed relationships, not binary joins

What survives is a typed edge — FK-join, lookup, many-to-many bridge, derived field, or unrelated-despite-similarity — carrying its evidence, its calibrated confidence, and a per-value provenance ledger you can audit.

Trust architecture

We never see your raw data.

A solo company can't out-attest a hyperscaler. So we out-architect them. With client-side anonymization, the customer holds the traceable-ID key and the cloud computes only on anonymized input.

The question stops being “do I trust their SOC 2?” and becomes “they cannot see my identifiable data.”

That's a mathematical answer to a procurement question — one a bundle-everything incumbent structurally can't match. It collapses much of the data-processing-agreement and DPIA burden for an EU-exposed buyer, and caps the blast radius of any breach to anonymized data.

  • Per-tenant learning stays isolated — one customer's join patterns never leak into another's.
  • Keyless and deterministic core: no API key required, no network at runtime.
  • Maps one-to-one to EU AI Act Article 10 data-provenance and as-of reconstruction.
